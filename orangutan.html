Orangutan

The orangutans are the two exclusively Asian species of extant great apes. Native to Indonesia and Malaysia, orangutans are currently found in only the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra. Scientific name: Pongo, Lifespan: 35 to 45 years. Gestation period: 274 days Height: Bornean orangutan: 3.9 to 4.6 ft. Mass: Bornean orangutan: 110 â€“ 220 lbs. The orangutan species are considered to be critically endangered. Human activities have caused severe declines in the populations and ranges of the species. Threats to wild orangutan populations include poaching, habitat destruction, and the illegal pet trade. Several conservation and rehabilitation organizations are dedicated to the survival of orangutans in the wild. Did you know: The name orangutan comes from two Malay words, orang which means person, and hutan which means forest; so orangutan means person of the forest.
