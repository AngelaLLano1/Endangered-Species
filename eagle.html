Eagle

Eagle is a common name for many large birds of prey of the family Accipitridae; it belongs to several groups of genera that are not closely related to each other. Most of the 60 species of eagles are from Eurasia and Africa. Speed: Bald eagle: 75 to 99 mph Wingspan: Bald eagle: 5.9 to 7.5 ft., Steller's sea eagle: 6.4 to 8.2 ft. Lifespan: Bald eagle: 20 years, Crowned eagle: 14 years Mass: Bald eagle: 6.6 to 14 lbs, Golden eagle: 11 lbs. Clutch size: Steller's sea eagle: 1 to 3, Crowned eagle: 1 to 2. Length: Golden eagle: 2.3 to 2.8 ft., Steller's sea eagle: 2.8 to 3.4 ft.
